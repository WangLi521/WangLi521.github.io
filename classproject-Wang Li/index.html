A Brief Introduction to AUM
  • Auburn University, founded in 1856 in the university town of Auburn, Alabama, is one of America's top 100 traditional universities.It's about an hour and a half's drive from Atlanta, the largest city in the Southeast.Founded in Montgomery in 1967, auburn has 5,408 stadium students.Auburn Offers degrees in more than 130 fields and offers the state's only publicly supported programs in many areas, including agriculture, forestry, architecture, architecture, pharmacy, and veterinary medicine.In particular, it has a strong research program in the schools of literature, science, mathematics, business, education, and Engineering and Auburn University Sports Program.Auburn University has a faculty of Agriculture, a Faculty of Architecture and Design, a Faculty of Engineering, a Faculty of Business, a Faculty of education, a Faculty of Forestry and Wildlife, a Faculty of anthropology, a Faculty of Arts, a Faculty of Nursing, a Faculty of Pharmacy, a Faculty of Veterinary Medicine, a Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, a Graduate School, and a Faculty of Honors.Auburn currently has 24,100 students enrolled, making it the state's largest campus.Auburn University is located in Auburn University Town, Alabama.

AUM Academics

  • The Warhawk Academic Success Center is home to the Learning Center and the Student Success and Retention Office. Working together, these two offices serve to support students and their academic success. Our mission is simple - supporting the academic success of students and their persistence towards graduation.
  • For more information regarding the Learning Center please visit the Learning Center webpage.
  • The Student Success and Retention office is home to the Bridge program, Undeclared Academic Advising, UNIV 1000, and Warhawk Warning.
